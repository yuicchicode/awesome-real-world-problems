# Parents lack clear visibility into the student’s real progress

## Problem summary
Many parents receive school information, but still cannot clearly understand how their child is actually progressing. Grades and occasional comments rarely provide enough context to support early intervention, meaningful help at home, or a real sense of the student’s learning journey.

## Real-world report
I do receive information from the school, but most of the time I still do not feel like I understand how my child is really doing. I may get grades, report cards, generic comments, and occasional messages, but that does not tell me where my child is improving, where they are struggling, or whether a problem is quietly getting worse.

Sometimes the numbers look acceptable, but the real issue is hidden somewhere else. My child may be keeping up just enough to avoid obvious alarms while still accumulating learning gaps, losing confidence, or struggling with reading, interpretation, focus, or study habits. In many cases, families only realize there is a serious issue when it has already grown too much.

The communication also tends to be too sparse or too generic. It feels like parents only hear something meaningful during formal meetings, report card delivery, or when the problem has already become serious. I want to help earlier, but I do not have clear, ongoing, actionable information.

## Why this is difficult
Student progress is multi-dimensional. Grades alone are not enough. Participation, consistency, recurring difficulties, engagement, learning behavior, and hidden gaps matter too.

## Why it matters
Without visibility, parents cannot support effectively, schools lose opportunities for early intervention, and students may fall behind silently.

## Project opportunity
This is a strong Project problem because it sits at the intersection of education, communication, analytics, and family engagement. It is especially good for contributors interested in dashboards, reporting, notifications, and learning support workflows.

## Technical requirements
- Support roles for parent, teacher, coordinator, and student
- Track progress beyond grades, including participation, recurring difficulties, and learning behavior
- Show trends over time instead of isolated results
- Surface early-warning patterns when a student’s performance declines
- Allow teachers to log quick observations without heavy overhead
- Present information in plain language for families
- Support class-level and student-level views
- Keep historical records for interventions and observations
- Allow filtering by subject, period, and type of difficulty
- Support privacy boundaries between roles

## Suggested MVP
- Student progress dashboard for parents
- Teacher observations feed
- Subject-level progress view
- Alerts for recurring low performance
- Timeline of notes and academic events

## Possible extensions
- Personalized parent guidance suggestions
- Study routine recommendations
- Learning-gap visualization
- Integration with school systems
- Multilingual family communication
