# Slow decision-making due to lack of centralized information

## Problem summary
In many foreign trade operations, the company technically has the information it needs, but that information is not organized in one centralized and trustworthy place. As a result, every important decision takes too long because people first need to reconstruct the situation before they can act.

## Real-world report
Another very serious problem is that, in many foreign trade processes, the company actually has the data it needs, but that data is not organized in one centralized and reliable place.

Some of the information is with the customs broker. Some is with the internal team. Some is buried in old emails. Some is inside spreadsheets. Some is in loose documents. Some is with suppliers or logistics partners. When someone needs to make a decision — such as approving a shipment, correcting a document, reacting to a delay, negotiating a deadline, or assessing cost impact — they first lose time trying to assemble the full picture before they can actually act.

In practice, nobody makes decisions quickly because the first step is always trying to understand what is really happening.

What is the current cargo status? Was the correct document already sent? Is there any customs issue still pending? Did the supplier confirm production? Did the shipment already leave? Has the carrier received the cargo? Did the deadline change? Which costs are already confirmed and which ones may still vary?

Those answers may technically exist, but they are not visible in a simple and consolidated way. Because of that, every decision turns into almost an investigation.

This becomes especially painful because foreign trade is an area where small delays in response can quickly turn into larger operational and financial consequences. When information is decentralized, the company reacts slowly, identifies problems too late, and takes too long even to align internally on what should happen next.

Instead of making decisions from a clear operational picture, people end up deciding with doubt, incomplete information, or while still waiting for confirmation from several different sides.

## Why this is difficult
The problem is not only missing data. The harder issue is fragmented visibility.

The operation contains many parallel details: shipment timing, document status, customs dependencies, supplier readiness, cost movement, logistics handoffs, and internal approvals. Even if each piece exists somewhere, the lack of one consolidated view makes the process slow, person-dependent, and hard to manage with confidence.

## Why it matters
This affects agility, autonomy, and operational control.

The team takes longer to identify problems, longer to respond, and longer to align internally. Planning becomes weaker, decision-making becomes reactive, and the business starts feeling like everything is always being solved through improvisation.

It also increases dependency on specific people. Very often, only the person who has followed the process for a long time knows where each piece of information lives or who needs to be contacted to unblock something. If that person is unavailable, the rest of the team loses speed and confidence immediately.

## Project opportunity
This is a strong Project problem because it represents a very common operational gap: the company does not necessarily lack information, it lacks centralized operational visibility.

A good solution here could help teams consolidate status, surface missing context, reduce dependency on scattered communication, and make trade decisions faster and safer. It is also a strong portfolio problem because it combines workflow orchestration, status modeling, role-based access, operational dashboards, exception handling, and decision-support logic.

## Technical requirements
- Support centralized tracking of shipment, documentation, customs, logistics, and internal approval status
- Consolidate process information from multiple stakeholders into one operational view
- Show current status, pending items, blockers, deadlines, and recent updates in one place
- Track cost visibility, including confirmed and still-variable cost components
- Allow attachment or reference of documents tied to specific process stages
- Support milestone-based views for decision-making
- Keep a historical log of changes, confirmations, and unresolved issues
- Surface missing or outdated information before it blocks action
- Reduce person-dependency by making context visible to the broader team
- Allow role-based visibility for internal teams, brokers, and partners
- Support search and filtering by shipment, date, partner, status, and issue type
- Provide a dashboard focused on operational decision readiness, not only raw process data

## Suggested MVP
- Central shipment status dashboard
- Pending and blocked-items panel
- Document and milestone tracker
- Cost visibility section
- Recent updates timeline
- Decision-readiness view for each process

## Possible extensions
- Approval workflows
- Alerting for deadline and customs risk
- Integration with email and spreadsheet imports
- Supplier and broker portal views
- Operational dependency mapping
- AI-assisted context summaries for faster decisions
