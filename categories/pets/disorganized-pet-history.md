# Disorganized pet history / lack of a centralized pet record

## Problem summary
A pet’s history often ends up scattered across memory, paper notes, chat messages, veterinary clinics, pet shops, exam providers, and different professionals. When something important happens, the caregiver may have pieces of information, but not one reliable and complete record of the animal’s health journey.

## Real-world report
One very common problem is that my pet’s history ends up spread everywhere. Some information stays in my memory, some is written on paper, some is buried in WhatsApp conversations, some is with the veterinary clinic, some is with the pet shop, and sometimes nobody has a full view of that animal’s life.

When I need that information the most, everything becomes harder. If I need to schedule an appointment, deal with an emergency, explain a behavioral change, or even switch professionals, I suddenly have to remember what happened before, which vaccines were already taken, which exams were done, what medications my pet used, whether there was any reaction, allergy, or recurring issue, and what the previous guidance was.

In practice, this creates a lot of insecurity. Sometimes I vaguely remember that my pet had a similar problem before, but I cannot remember when it happened, which treatment was used, or what the vet said at the time. In other situations, the animal has been seen in multiple places — clinic, grooming service, specialist, lab exams — and each interaction generated useful information, but none of it was really centralized.

That means every new appointment starts to feel like starting from zero. The professional depends on my memory, and I do not always have the full picture available when I need it. Important patterns can get lost simply because the information is not accessible in a clear and usable way.

## Why this is difficult
The problem is not only storing pet data. The difficult part is maintaining continuity across many different moments, providers, and contexts.

A pet may receive care from more than one clinic, more than one professional, and more than one service provider. Some records are formal, some are informal, and some only exist in memory. Over time, the information becomes fragmented, inconsistent, and hard to retrieve quickly.

## Why it matters
When the pet history is disorganized, decision-making becomes weaker and care becomes more fragmented.

That affects the quality of follow-up, reduces visibility into recurring issues, increases dependence on memory, and makes urgent situations more stressful than they need to be. It also makes it harder for a new professional to understand the animal’s actual background.

## Project opportunity
This is a strong Project problem because it is practical, relatable, and easy for the community to understand.

A good solution here could help pet caregivers centralize records, preserve care continuity, and make important information usable across time. It is also a strong portfolio problem because it combines records, timelines, attachments, reminders, role-based data, and real-world workflows involving trust and continuity.

## Technical requirements
- Support one or more pets under the same caregiver account
- Store basic pet profile data such as species, breed, age, weight, and identification details
- Keep a chronological history of appointments, exams, vaccines, medications, symptoms, allergies, reactions, and observations
- Allow attachments such as exam results, prescriptions, vaccination cards, and photos
- Support notes from multiple types of care interactions
- Preserve historical data without overwriting past records
- Allow quick search and filtering by event type, date, symptom, medication, or provider
- Support fields for recurring conditions and long-term observations
- Make the record easy to access on mobile, especially in urgent situations
- Allow optional sharing of selected history with veterinarians, clinics, or caregivers
- Support incomplete records without breaking the timeline
- Keep the structure flexible enough for different animals and care routines

## Suggested MVP
- Pet profile page
- Timeline of medical and care events
- Vaccine and medication record
- Exam and prescription attachments
- Searchable notes history
- Basic reminders for future care dates

## Possible extensions
- Shared access with family members or caretakers
- Veterinary portal view
- Symptom pattern detection
- Emergency summary mode
- Multi-clinic record import
- AI-generated care summaries
