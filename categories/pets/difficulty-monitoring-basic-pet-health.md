# Difficulty monitoring basic pet health

## Problem summary
Basic pet health is often monitored in a reactive way instead of a continuous one. Many caregivers only take action when something obvious happens, while small changes in routine, behavior, appetite, energy, hydration, medication, or preventive care go untracked until the problem becomes more serious.

## Real-world report
Another important problem is that my pet’s basic health is usually not followed in a continuous way. Most of the time, I only look for help when something more visible happens, when a vaccine date arrives, when the animal gets sick, or when there is a clear sign that something is wrong.

But between those moments, there is very little structured follow-up of the basics: food, weight, water intake, energy level, mood, behavior, vet visits, deworming, flea prevention, exam follow-ups, and all the small daily signals that might indicate that something is changing.

The hard part is that many changes begin in subtle ways. My pet may start drinking more water than usual, eating less, sleeping differently, staying quieter, losing or gaining weight, changing habits, or showing small discomforts that seem harmless when seen in isolation. Because there is no simple and consistent monitoring process, these changes either go unnoticed or are treated as something temporary.

Preventive care also becomes fragile. I may truly want to take good care of the animal, but in the middle of daily life I forget vaccine dates, medication timing, prevention boosters, exam returns, or the signs I should be paying more attention to. Basic care ends up depending too much on memory, urgency, and good intentions instead of a structured routine.

That creates guilt and insecurity. I feel like I could be tracking things better, but I do not want care to become something complicated, full of spreadsheets and manual overhead. At the same time, I do not want to realize too late that I ignored signals that mattered.

## Why this is difficult
The challenge is not only knowing what should be monitored. It is turning preventive care into something simple enough to sustain over time.

Basic health signals are subtle, easy to dismiss, and often only become meaningful when seen as a pattern. A system that is too heavy will be abandoned, but a system that is too shallow will miss what matters.

## Why it matters
When basic monitoring is weak, problems are noticed late, preventive care becomes inconsistent, and the caregiver has less confidence in everyday health decisions.

This affects the pet’s well-being, increases reactivity, and turns routine care into something that only happens once a problem is already visible.

## Open-source opportunity
This is a strong open-source problem because it sits between prevention, habit formation, lightweight tracking, reminders, and simple health signals.

It is especially valuable as an open-source project because it can be built in a practical and modular way: daily check-ins, reminders, logs, alerts, historical trends, and optional integrations with more advanced health workflows later.

## Technical requirements
- Support daily or periodic tracking of basic health indicators such as appetite, weight, water intake, behavior, energy, sleep, and bowel habits
- Allow customizable preventive-care schedules for vaccines, deworming, flea/tick prevention, medications, and check-ups
- Keep a chronological timeline of routine observations
- Support reminders and recurring tasks without creating too much friction
- Make it easy to log small observations quickly from mobile
- Allow notes about unusual behavior or mild symptoms
- Surface trends over time instead of only isolated entries
- Highlight missing follow-up items or overdue preventive actions
- Support multiple pets with different routines and schedules
- Allow caregiver-friendly summaries instead of overly technical reports
- Keep data entry lightweight enough for long-term consistency
- Support optional export or sharing of recent health history

## Suggested MVP
- Pet health dashboard
- Quick daily check-in
- Weight and routine tracker
- Preventive care reminders
- Timeline of observations
- Overdue care alerts

## Possible extensions
- Pattern-based alerts for behavior or appetite changes
- Shared household caregiving
- Veterinary follow-up mode
- Wearable or smart-device integration
- AI-assisted trend summaries
- Health calendar view
