# Local Commerce

This category includes real-world problems related to small stores, neighborhood businesses, and local commercial operations.

These problems often affect store owners, employees, and customers. They usually involve simple but critical operational needs such as stock control, daily workflow organization, owner overload, customer service, and low-process environments.

Typical topics in this category include:

- inventory control
- store operations
- owner overload
- small business coordination
- day-to-day commerce workflows
- basic operational visibility

This category is a good fit for problems where a local business works mainly through effort, memory, and improvisation instead of clear systems and reliable structure.

## Examples
- No inventory control
- The owner doing everything alone
