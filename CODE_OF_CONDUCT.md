# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We want this project to be a welcoming, respectful, and constructive space for everyone.

This repository exists to help people share, discuss, and build around real-world problems. That only works well when contributors feel safe to participate, ask questions, suggest ideas, and improve the project without fear of hostility, disrespect, or exclusion.

We are committed to making participation in this project a positive experience for everyone, regardless of experience level, background, identity, personal characteristics, nationality, language, religion, disability, or any other protected characteristic.

---

## Expected behavior

We expect contributors and participants to:

- Be respectful and considerate in discussions
- Assume good intent, especially in early contributions
- Give feedback in a constructive and specific way
- Focus on improving ideas, writing, structure, and clarity
- Be welcoming to new contributors
- Respect different perspectives and real-world contexts
- Help keep discussions practical, useful, and collaborative
- Accept feedback gracefully
- Communicate clearly and professionally

---

## Unacceptable behavior

Unacceptable behavior includes, but is not limited to:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination of any kind
- Personal attacks, insults, or hostile language
- Trolling, baiting, or deliberately disruptive behavior
- Dismissing others in a demeaning or aggressive way
- Sexual language or unwanted sexual attention
- Publishing private information without permission
- Repeatedly ignoring project guidelines in bad faith
- Gatekeeping, mockery, or humiliation of new contributors
- Derailing discussions into unnecessary conflict
- Any conduct that would make participation feel unsafe or exclusionary

---

## Project-specific expectations

Because this repository is centered on real-world problems, we ask contributors to be especially thoughtful when discussing:

- health-related situations
- education and children
- nonprofit and community work
- family and caregiving routines
- hiring, employment, and retention
- any context involving vulnerable people or sensitive experiences

Please avoid dismissive, cynical, or dehumanizing language when describing these problems.

The goal of this project is to make real pain points easier to understand and build around, not to reduce people’s experiences to stereotypes, jokes, or shallow assumptions.

---

## Feedback and disagreements

Disagreement is normal in collaborative projects.

If you disagree with a contribution, comment, or proposal:

- focus on the content, not the person
- explain your reasoning clearly
- suggest a better alternative when possible
- avoid sarcasm, contempt, or unnecessary harshness

Thoughtful disagreement is welcome.
Hostile behavior is not.

---

## Maintainer responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this Code of Conduct.

Maintainers may remove, edit, or reject comments, issues, pull requests, discussions, or other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct.

Maintainers are expected to act fairly, proportionally, and in the best interest of the community and the project.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces, including:

- issues
- pull requests
- discussions
- documentation reviews
- community conversations related to this repository

It also applies when someone is representing the project in public spaces.

---

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it to the project maintainers.

When reporting, include as much relevant context as possible, such as:

- what happened
- where it happened
- who was involved
- links, screenshots, or references if available

All reports should be handled seriously and respectfully.

---

## Enforcement

Maintainers may take any action they consider appropriate in response to unacceptable behavior, including:

- private warning
- public warning
- content removal
- temporary restriction from participation
- permanent ban from the project

The response will depend on the severity, pattern, and context of the behavior.

---

## Good-faith participation

We recognize that not every mistake is malicious.

Contributors who act in good faith, listen to feedback, and adjust their behavior should be met with clarity and fairness.

This project values improvement, learning, and respectful collaboration.

---

## Final note

This repository is meant to be useful, practical, and open to contribution.

A healthy community is part of that usefulness.

Please help make this project a place where people can contribute thoughtfully, learn publicly, and build meaningful open-source work together.
